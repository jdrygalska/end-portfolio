R&D internship focused on AI-based declustering for Qcify AIR

R&D · Computer Vision · Industrial AI

R&D Project Intern - Qcify

February 2026 — May 2026

During my internship at Qcify, I worked on improving declustering for the Qcify AIR machine, an industrial optical sorting system used in inline food processing. The project focused on separating touching or closely positioned almonds in segmentation outputs while preserving enough object information for later classification and sorting decisions.

My key responsibilities included:

  • Analysed previous experimental approaches and researched segmentation strategies for almond declustering.
  • Prepared datasets with boundary masks and distance maps for 32 x 3072 and 64 x 3072 input configurations.
  • Trained and compared lightweight semantic segmentation models, mainly ESPNetV2, with an additional FBSNet experiment.
  • Implemented and evaluated loss functions, boundary supervision, distance-map targets and penalty terms.
  • Compared model performance using IoU, object-counting metrics, merge/split rates, runtime measurements and visual inspection.

This internship gave me valuable hands-on experience in computer vision, R&D experimentation, model evaluation and the practical constraints of applying AI in a real-time industrial machine environment.
